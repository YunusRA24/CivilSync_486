Mike Duggan Stances Summary
Prepared for CivilSync
Updated: April 14, 2026

Note:
This is a concise summary of Mike Duggan's publicly stated positions and campaign messaging. On some issues—especially healthcare and immigration/border policy—his stance is not presented as a detailed formal platform, so the summary reflects what he has said publicly rather than a full campaign plan.

1. Healthcare
Mike Duggan has not released a highly detailed standalone healthcare platform on his campaign site, but his public comments suggest that he sees healthcare mainly through cost control, system coordination, and chronic disease management. He has said that Michigan needs to reduce healthcare costs by managing chronic illnesses better, cutting paperwork, and making the different parts of the healthcare system—such as hospitals, pharmacies, and insurers—work together more effectively. He also points to his years running the Detroit Medical Center as proof that he can cut waste and manage large health systems.

At the same time, Duggan drew criticism in 2025 for defending Medicaid work requirements and downplaying the impact of federal Medicaid cuts. That means his healthcare stance appears more centered on efficiency and administration than on expanding public coverage.

2. Gun Rights / Guns
Duggan's public position is more focused on reducing gun violence than on expanding gun rights. He has praised laws aimed at taking guns away from people who are considered a threat, supported coordinated law-enforcement efforts to remove illegal guns from the streets, and backed community-based violence prevention programs in Detroit.

He has also said that simply creating "gun-free zones" may not do much by itself to reduce shootings. So overall, his stance reads as: support for public-safety-oriented gun regulation and enforcement, with a strong emphasis on illegal guns and violence prevention rather than a broad pro-Second-Amendment message.

3. Abortion
Duggan has publicly supported preserving abortion rights in Michigan. In 2022, when Michigan officials were fighting over whether abortion protections would survive the fall of Roe v. Wade, he supported Gov. Gretchen Whitmer's legal effort to protect abortion access and said he believed most people in Michigan wanted that right preserved.

After the Supreme Court overturned Roe, Duggan also described the ruling as an attack on constitutional rights that threatened access to safe healthcare services. Based on his public statements, he should be categorized as supportive of abortion rights rather than supportive of abortion restrictions.

4. The Border / Immigration
Duggan does not appear to have a detailed standalone statewide "border" platform as a gubernatorial candidate. The clearest public signals come from his comments about immigration enforcement in Detroit. He has said Detroit is not a sanctuary city and that the city would comply with federal immigration policy. That suggests a relatively moderate or enforcement-accommodating position rather than a sanctuary-style posture.

At the same time, Duggan has also acknowledged that immigration raids cause real fear in communities, saying that "the pain when ICE is in your community is very real." More recent reporting also noted that he declined to spell out a more detailed policy for how he would handle federal immigration enforcement if elected governor. So the best summary is that he sounds sympathetic to immigrant communities, but he has not clearly committed to a strong anti-enforcement or pro-sanctuary position.

5. Jobs
Jobs and economic growth are among Duggan's clearest and strongest issues. His campaign repeatedly emphasizes business growth, workforce development, neighborhood investment, and opportunity for working families. He points to his Detroit record—especially attracting investment, growing small businesses, expanding affordable housing, and supporting entrepreneurship—as proof that he can create jobs.

One of his most specific examples is Motor City Match, which he says helped open nearly 200 businesses and create more than 1,700 jobs. In campaign messaging, Duggan presents himself as a pragmatic economic manager focused on statewide recovery, workforce development, reducing barriers for business growth, and creating more opportunity outside partisan politics.

Healthcare: Cost-control / system-efficiency / chronic-disease-management focus; not strongly framed as coverage expansion.
Gun Rights: More gun-violence-prevention and illegal-gun enforcement than gun-rights expansion.
Abortion: Supports preserving abortion rights.
Border / Immigration: Moderate and somewhat ambiguous; not sanctuary-oriented, but also not clearly hardline.
Jobs: Strongly pro-job growth, workforce development, and small-business/economic expansion.
