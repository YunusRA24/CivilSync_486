Michigan created the middle class and put the world on wheels. We pride ourselves on our innovation, our resilience, and our hardworking communities. But as I listen to people around our state, it's clear that too many families are struggling to make ends meet. And too often, government is showing up everywhere we don't need it and nowhere where we do.


Take the rising cost of housing as an example. I've met with families across Michigan who are being priced out of their own neighborhoods, young people who can't afford their first home, and seniors on fixed incomes facing skyrocketing property taxes. As a working mother, I understand what it means to balance a household budget and worry about whether you can afford to stay in the community you love. It's past time we root out the corruption and inefficiency holding us back and build a government that truly works for people—a government that makes life more affordable for families, from housing to healthcare to childcare.

My Affordable Michigan agenda will tackle the costs hitting families hardest. I will work to increase housing availability and affordability by streamlining regulations that slow down construction, incentivizing development of starter homes and affordable units, and cracking down on corporate investors who buy up properties and drive up rents. I will fight to lower healthcare costs by increasing transparency in medical billing, passing a $5,000 caregiver tax credit, and ensuring that pharmaceutical companies can't price gouge Michigan families. And I will make childcare more accessible and affordable by bringing Rx Kids statewide and removing barriers that prevent qualified providers from opening their doors.

From the grocery store to the gas pump, from the doctor's office to the housing market, I will work constantly as your governor to bring down costs and protect families from corporate greed. I know what it takes to fix broken systems because I've done it before. Now I'm ready to do that across all of state government, ensuring everyone who works hard can afford to live, thrive, and build a future right here in Michigan.

No matter where I go in Michigan, the high cost of housing is one of the biggest
issues people bring up. In Traverse City, farmers can't find a place to raise their
families when homes cost nearly a million dollars. In Detroit, families who have lived
in the city for generations are being priced out by skyrocketing rents. The problem
is clear: we don't build enough and our property taxes are too high. We need to
build more quality housing – efficiently and effectively.

Childcare


We have a childcare affordability crisis in Michigan. Some parents are stuck on waiting lists for months; others are paying more for care than their mortgage. And too often, women are pushed out of the workforce because affordable, reliable care simply doesn't exist. As a working mom and public servant, I understand the stress, the tradeoffs, and the urgency families feel every single day.

The crisis runs deeper than most people realize. Right now, childcare workers earn poverty wages while doing some of the most important work there is — and they're leaving the field in droves. We can't build a stable system on an unstable workforce. When the people caring for our youngest children can't afford childcare for their own kids, can't pay rent on their salaries, and have no clear career pathway, the whole system collapses. That's why waiting lists keep growing, and costs keep climbing. We simply don't have enough providers because we've failed to treat early childhood education as the dignified profession it is. 

We can and must build a system that supports children's development, respects working parents, and provides every child a strong start from day one.

Imagine a Michigan where every family has access to next-generation early learning and care that's affordable, reliable, and built to last. That's the future I'm ready to deliver.

I grew up in a middle-class family and know how hard so many families are working just to
get by and pay the bills. And as those bills continue to go up instead of down, we need
urgent solutions to turn this around. Because Michiganders pay some of the highest heat
and electricity bills in the nation while enduring an antiquated, unreliable grid rife with
failures and power outages – all while monopoly utility companies make record profits. It’s
unfair, corrupt, and it has to end.
As governor, I will not allow publicly-owned and regulated utilities to increase your bills
while neglecting improvements to our power grid. Because when we, as Michiganders,
guarantee a profit for these companies, there must be accountability. When storms knock
out power for days, people deserve fair compensation. As governor, I will stand with
Michiganders who are fed up with the choice of paying their electric bill or their groceries.
We need to work together to modernize our power grid and drive down energy costs. As
Governor I will use every tool in my toolbox to get this done, and hold utility companies
accountable for the service we all need.


End unfair profit-driven rate hikes and implement a transparent,
accountable rate-setting process that puts people — and grid reliability —
before profits. We can't allow endless increases to our energy bills while
public utilities deliver unreliable, unaffordable service. I will ban rate
increases that are only being used to pad profits, I will increase outage
credits for residents, and institute performance-based profit standards so
utilities only earn maximum returns when they are delivering reliable,
affordable service to Michiganders.
